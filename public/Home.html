POST-CONSTRUCTION CLEANING +
Imagine you've just finished a big home renovation project. There's dust and debris everywhere and it seems like a big task to make it clean and livable again. That's where post-construction cleaning comes in. This involves removing all that construction waste, dusting every corner, scrubbing your floors and walls, sanitizing your bathrooms and kitchens, and making sure every window and door is spotless. It's a thorough cleaning to make your new space ready for you to enjoy.
PERIODIC CLEANING +
Think about those cleaning tasks that you don't need to do every day, but every once in a while. Maybe it's washing the windows, dusting off the blinds, getting that oven sparkling again, or giving the carpets a good shampooing. These are tasks that you might do weekly, monthly, or even seasonally. It's about maintaining the cleanliness of your space over time and preventing the build-up of dust and grime.
GENERAL CLEANING +
This is your everyday cleaning routine. It's sweeping the floors, mopping up any spills, dusting off surfaces, cleaning the bathrooms and kitchen, and tidying up any clutter. It's about keeping your home or workplace clean on a regular basis, helping to prevent the spread of germs and creating a comfortable environment for you and your family or colleagues.
STANDARD CLEANING +
You might also hear this referred to as regular cleaning. It's a bit like your general cleaning, but it's often a set package of services offered by professional cleaning companies. This could include cleaning bathrooms, wiping down kitchen surfaces, dusting, vacuuming, mopping, and general tidying. While it's a thorough clean, it doesn't usually include more in-depth tasks like washing windows or deep-cleaning carpets. Those would typically fall under a deep cleaning or spring cleaning service.
